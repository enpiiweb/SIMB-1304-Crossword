A SEASONAL TREAT by Stroz

The solutions and explanations to the 2001 PUD are as follows:

1. Silage (anag of 'gasoline' minus ('no) 'no') 2. Slater (= roofer) 3. Gender (Remove (or top) the first letters of 'Age', 'and' and 'her'). 4. Gather (Fish = 'gar' around 'The' = French tea) 5. Tether (The word 'Ufuifs' contains the letters of the alphabet which follow the light (or answer) Tether) 6. Debase (paper-cutter = editor = ed (reversed) = 'de' + 'home' = base) 7. Rebate (Back 'er' = re + English = e + TAB (reversed) = betting shop) 8. Heaste (Remove first letters from (behead) 'She' and 'was' = he as + TE = TE Lawrence (British writer): Spenserian word for "command") 9. Aerate (Homophone of "the cost of flying") 10. Repast (feed = repast; 'back?' suggests a meal in the [re]'past') 11. Stasis (anag of 'assist') 12. Seethe (See = "Come to" + 'the' = boil) 13. Rehire (Note = re + let = hire: 'do' = ditto = hire again = rehire) 14. Recipe (French grocer = epicier minus 'moi' = 'I' reversed) 15. Cytisi (anag of 'City is') 16. Samiti (Remove ('endlessly') the last letters of 'sat', 'me', 'in', 'to', and 'it') 17. Seamer (English water = sea + French water = mer) 18. Seeger ('To observe' = see + 'Reg' (Ansett) reversed) 19. Entire (Entre = French for 'between' with 'I' inserted ('in') 20 Acuity (Add 'v' for 'volume' = vacuity = 'emptiness') 21. E-layer (E = Ecstasy (drugs) + 'egg producer' (hen) = layer) 22. Retene (anag 'entree') 23. Hecate (quotation) 24. Reduce (double definition; to thicken a sauce by boiling) 25. Denial (Hidden (accommodation) in last six letters (reversed = about) of 'complained') 26. Hether (Homophone of 'Erica' = 'heather' = Spenserian word for 'here') 27. Rather (Heel = 'rat' add (wears) 'she' = 'her') 28. Warren (insert ('to drive into') a supercar = a Rolls-Royce = ARR in 'wen' = London (see Chambers 1998 ed, p1896) (Earl Warren (1891-1974) American politician and judge) 29. Negate ("No Way" = 'negative' minus ('Out') 'four' = iv) 30. Alegar (A member = a leg + a recipe = 'a r'. Sentiment: See the Lights in the New Year. (The sentiment: "The Lights see the New Year in" was not accepted as the letters of this phrase are not "in a certain order" as required.) Many thanks for the helpful comments.
