E-Lodgment of Entries

The Australian Crossword Club accepts electronically-transmitted entries for the competitions for its primary puzzles (Slots 1-5). Such entries are not to be sent to the nominated Adjudicator for the month, but to Ian Williams whose e-mail address is:

ianw@webone.com.au

E-lodgement saves postage costs and avoids postal delays and non-delivery. It can also give you a receipt for the delivery of the e-mail message. And there’s more! It can even help you proof-read your solutions.

The Closing time for e-submissions is the same time as for postal entries on the date published each month in Crozworld.

Preparing crosswords for e-lodgement 

Crosswords for e-lodgement can be prepared using Doug Butler’s AxWord. You can download a "light" version of AxWord from the link in Doug’s article above. This version is sufficient to enable anyone to prepare crosswords in a convenient and transportable form. It has all the requisites for composing on screen, printing and saving, etc. - it just doesn't have the word lists and search capabilities of the full version of Axword. 

To e-lodge, you can simply attach a file or files prepared from the solutions you have completed in AxWord as follows: 

Having previously entered and saved (in C:\Program files\Axword, or any other directory in which you wish to save the puzzles) each of the puzzles that you wish to submit, start AxWord. In the AxWord search page click on “File” and "Batch Functions" and navigate to the directory containing the solved puzzles. Assemble the list of 5, or however many solutions you want to submit, by highlighting the file name and clicking on "Add to Batch". Select “Make file Desktop\temp.xwd” from the drop-down list on the left of the Batch Functions pane [the first option is “Print Solutions 3 wide”] and click on "Run Batch" All of the solutions you have selected will now be included in the file temp.xwd, which will be placed automatically on your Desktop.

In your e-mail program, commence a new e-mail to Ian Williams (ianw@webone.com.au) and attach it to the e-mail. Alternatively, copy the file from the Desktop and attach it to the e-mail message by pasting. If you wish to receive blank grids for each month’s puzzles, e-mail Ian and ask to be included on the distribution list. More comprehensive advice is included with the monthly e-mail to help you process and find the relevant file(s) and attach them.

Some Frequently Asked Questions about e-lodgement 

Q: Must I submit solutions to all 5 puzzles?
A: No, only as many as you want adjudicated 

Q: In any order?
A: In any order. 

Q: Can I submit, say 2 this week and 2 next week?
A: If you wish. 

Q: Can I submit the Clue of the Month or any other comment in the e-mail message?
A: Yes, all additional material is passed on to the adjudicator and handled as if it had been submitted by ordinary mail. 

Q: Can I snail-mail some to the adjudicator of the month and e-submit others?
A: No, sorry. It complicates the adjudication no end! 

Q: Can I e-submit Slot 6 puzzles?
A: No – they should be sent direct to the adjudicator of Slot 6 puzzles, who will normally be the setter. 

Q: Can I e-submit one month and snail-mail the next?
A: Yes. 

Q: Are e-mailers included in the prize draws?
A: Yes. 

Q: Do I need AxWord to compile the batch of solutions?
A: It’s preferable if you do it in the way described above.

Q: What if the grid is not compatible with Axword or a conventional grid, eg a hexapentagonal or other unusual diagram?
A Write the clue numbers/letters and the solutions in any consistent format. The submission will be assessed as it would be if it were sent by mail. 

Q: Will Ian Williams [ianw@webone.com.au] be e-judicator each month?
A: Yes, until further notice. Any changes will be announced in Crozworld.
